# HikRAD — one-page operator guide

*Print this. A new front-desk operator should be productive within an hour
using only this page (NFR-5).*

## Logging in

Go to the panel URL your admin gave you. Enter your username and password.
If your account has 2FA enabled, enter the 6-digit code from your
authenticator app.

## Find a subscriber

Use the search box at the top of every screen (keyboard shortcut: just
start typing from anywhere). Search by username, name, or phone number.
Click the result to open their profile — **1 click** from anywhere in the
panel.

## Renew a subscriber (most common task)

1. Find the subscriber (above).
2. Click **Renew**.
3. Confirm the plan/amount, take payment, click **Confirm**.

That's **3 clicks** from the dashboard. The subscriber's speed is restored
automatically — you never need to touch the router.

## Take a wallet top-up or scratch-card payment

1. Find the subscriber.
2. Click **Top Up**, choose cash / scratch card / e-wallet.
3. Enter the amount (or scan the scratch card code), click **Confirm**.

## Reset a subscriber's MAC address

If a subscriber changed routers/devices and their profile has MAC locking
on, they'll be rejected until you reset it:

1. Find the subscriber.
2. Click **Reset MAC** on their profile page.
3. Confirm.

They can reconnect with their new device immediately — no router changes
needed.

## Checking if someone is currently online

Subscriber profile page shows a **live status** badge (online/offline) and,
if online, their current NAS, IP, and session duration. The **Live
Sessions** screen shows everyone online right now, searchable the same way.

## Something looks wrong — who do I tell?

If a subscriber says they're not getting speed after renewal, or a NAS
shows as down, check the **Dashboard** for red/yellow alerts first — most
issues surface there before a subscriber calls. Escalate to your network
engineer if the dashboard itself shows a problem, not just one subscriber.

## What you can't do (and why)

Front-desk accounts don't see refunds, subscriber deletion, NAS/router
configuration, or settings — those need a manager or admin login. If you
need one of those, ask your supervisor rather than trying to work around it;
every action here is logged with your name and the time, on purpose (audit
log, so mistakes are traceable and fixable, not blamed on the wrong person).
